Science
Trading floors and the mind under pressure

High-frequency decision-making under financial stress produces measurable cognitive degradation — elevated cortisol, attentional narrowing, working memory impairment, and emotional dysregulation that compound across the session. The trader who has been at his desk for six hours is not the trader who sat down at the open. This was the problem we set out to instrument.

Neural signatures of cognitive error

EEG offers a window into the electrical activity of the brain at millisecond resolution. Specific frequency band signatures — elevated theta, suppressed alpha, frontal asymmetry in beta — are known correlates of stress-induced decision degradation. We built signal processing pipelines to detect these signatures in real time on a live trading desk, with the goal of providing the trader a closed-loop feedback intervention before the degraded decision was made.

The headset

We engineered a wearable EEG device calibrated for the trading environment: low-profile, minimal electrode footprint, robust to movement artifact and electromagnetic interference from trading floor infrastructure. The processing pipeline ran on-device at sub-200ms latency. We were proud of what we built. The science reached a place none of us had expected in the time we had.

The Lab
Eight institutions

The founding team was drawn from Stanford University, Princeton University, the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, the California Institute of Technology, UC Berkeley, UCLA, the University of Washington, and Boston University — researchers at different stages of their careers, across neuroscience, computer science, electrical engineering, and medicine, united by the belief that cognitive error on the trading floor was both measurable and addressable.

Eight months

We operated for eight months. The research reached a place we were genuinely proud of — the science held, the hardware worked, and the signal processing pipeline performed beyond what we had modeled in early design. We could not secure the funding the work required. We could not find the lab testers willing to let us sit behind them on a live desk. The combination was fatal. We closed.

Where the team went

The founding researchers dispersed across medicine and artificial intelligence. They went to clinical research programs, to frontier AI laboratories, to doctoral work at the institutions where they had trained. They are still there — still at the edge of what is known in their respective fields. The product failed. The researchers did not. We are getting back together.
